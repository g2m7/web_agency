## Market Reading

The competitive field breaks into four buckets:

1. DIY builders: `Wix`, `Squarespace`, `GoDaddy`, `WordPress.com`
2. AI builders: `Durable`, `10Web`, `Hostinger`
3. Agency platforms: `Duda`
4. Managed local-marketing providers: `Hibu`, `Townsquare`, plus full-service agencies like `WebFX`

Your real competition is not “everyone with a website tool.” It is:

- any cheap DIY option for price-sensitive owners,
- any local marketing agency that bundles websites with SEO / ads,
- any AI builder that lets an owner get “good enough” in an afternoon.

## What Competitors Miss That You Can Cover

### 1. Niche-specific, audit-led sales motion

Most builders start from “pick a template.” Your model starts from “here is the exact website issue hurting this business.”

### 2. Done-for-you execution without agency pricing

DIY tools are cheaper but shift work to the owner. Agencies remove the work but are expensive. Your slot is the middle.

### 3. Productized local-service website operations

Static deployment, one JSON content source, human review gates, and policy enforcement are operational advantages, not marketing fluff.

### 4. Discipline around promises

Most local agencies over-promise. Your policy engine can enforce no guarantees, no discounts, and clean scope boundaries. That matters.

## Hard Truth

The low end of this market is already commoditized. If your offer looks like “we also build small-business websites,” `Durable`, `Wix`, and `Hostinger` will crush you on perceived simplicity. You only win when the offer is:

- niche-specific,
- audit-led,
- managed,
- fast,
- and visibly safer than freelancers or generic builders.
