## Key Findings

1. The market is real, but the easy money is gone. SMB website adoption is already high at 83%, so your greenfield opportunity is the 17% still offline plus the much larger redesign/weak-site market. This is not a “sell any site to any small business” market anymore.
2. Your best wedge is not HVAC/plumbing/roofing first. Those markets are large, but they are also heavily agency-saturated. The better first-wave niches are service-area businesses with high lead value and visibly weak sites: pool service, tree service, garage door, landscaping, and selective pest control.
3. Your current pricing is below the managed-service market and above DIY software, which is the right lane for early validation. The mistake would be going cheaper. The pressure is to enforce the launch fee and keep pricing at the top of your current ranges, not cut rates.
4. Google is not killing websites, but it is reducing lazy brochure-site value. Discovery is shifting toward Google Maps, reviews, and AI-assisted search. That means your offer has to be “managed conversion layer for local search,” not “5 pages for cheap.”
5. The business is viable only if it stays narrow and disciplined. The main failure modes are bad niche choice, deliverability/compliance mistakes on first outreach, and overreliance on Google Maps scraping before you have a compliant replacement path.

## Critical Risks

- **Immediate:** burning domain reputation with weak cold outreach before you have a compliant outbound setup.
- **Near-term:** picking saturated city+niche pairs and concluding the whole model does not work.
- **Medium-term:** staying dependent on Google Maps scraping after validation volume rises.
- **Structural:** selling a low-ticket plan mix that creates support load faster than MRR.

## Top Recommendations

1. Start with three pairs only: `Phoenix + pool service`, `Charlotte + tree service`, and `Raleigh + garage door repair`.
2. Keep pricing at `Starter $79`, `Growth $129`, `Pro $249`, and stop treating the launch fee as optional after the first pilot batch.
3. Protect outreach from day one: separate sending domain, suppression list, physical address, clean opt-out handling, and manual approval until reply quality is proven.
4. Treat Google Maps scraping as a validation-stage input, not a permanent operating dependency.
5. Position the offer around speed, conversion, and managed ownership. Do not position it as “cheap websites.”

## Revenue Outlook

Base case, this is a slow-compounding outbound business, not an overnight spike. A realistic early funnel is roughly `1,000 validated leads -> 30-50 replies -> 8-15 demos -> 2-4 paid clients`. With a `Growth-heavy` mix, `$1K MRR` is reachable with `7-8 active clients`, `$5K MRR` with `35-40`, and `$10K MRR` with roughly `70` unless you move upmarket.
