## Conclusion

You are not overpriced. If anything, you are **underpriced for a managed service**.

## Should You Adjust Pricing Up or Down?

### Short answer

- **Do not lower pricing.**
- **Do not broaden the price range downward.**
- **Keep using the top of your current range.**

### Why

Lower pricing will do three bad things:

1. attract smaller, needier, higher-churn clients,
2. make it harder to absorb manual review / support load,
3. move you closer to DIY-builder comparison shopping, which is the worst battlefield for you.

### What to change instead

1. Stop treating the launch fee as optional after the first pilot clients.
2. Push mix toward `Growth`, not `Starter`.
3. Keep `Starter` as an entry plan, but do not let it become the default closed deal.

## Recommended Pricing Posture

### Next 30 days

- Keep `Starter $79`, `Growth $129`, `Pro $249`.
- Enforce the launch fee for any client after the first `3` pilot closes.
- Position `Growth` as the default best-fit plan.

### After first proof points

If you reach at least:

- `>5%` reply rate,
- `>25%` demo-to-close,
- low early churn,

then test:

- `Growth $149/mo`
- `Pro $299/mo`

I would **not** raise `Starter` first. I would raise the mid-tier and upper tier first because they align better with businesses that actually value speed and ongoing edits.

## Pricing Experiments to Run First

| Experiment | Test | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| Launch fee enforcement | `waived` vs `required` | Protects operator time and filters bad-fit buyers |
| Growth anchor | `Growth $129` vs `Growth $149` | Tells you whether demand is price-sensitive or proof-sensitive |
| Plan mix framing | lead with `Growth` vs lead with `Starter` | Determines whether you can steer clients upmarket without discounting |
| Offer framing | `managed website` vs `website + AI chat + ongoing updates` | Buyers often pay more for outcomes than for page counts |
| Edit-pack upsell | included limit only vs pre-priced add-on packs | Raises ARPA without raising base price too early |

## Brutal Truth

At your current numbers, the risk is not “too expensive.” The risk is building a book of low-ticket clients that feel cheap at sale time and expensive at support time.

That is why the correct move is:

- tighter niche focus,
- cleaner qualification,
- enforced launch fee,
- and eventually a higher `Growth` price once proof exists.
