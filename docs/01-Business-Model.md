# Business Model

## Core model

The business sells a managed website retainer to small businesses with weak or outdated websites. It is operated primarily by an AI agent that runs 24/7, with a human operator handling only tasks the agent cannot do (payment setup, legal, escalations, final quality checks).

The workflow is:

1. **Agent** identifies local businesses inside a chosen niche and geography.
2. **Agent** audits their website and digital presence.
3. **Agent** reaches out asynchronously by email — highlighting one visible issue and offering to show a better version.
4. **Agent** handles replies, classifies intent, and triggers next steps.
5. If the prospect requests a demo, **agent** builds a tailored mockup and sends it with pricing.
6. **Agent** closes the client onto a monthly subscription via hosted checkout link.
7. **Agent** delivers hosting, managed updates, analytics visibility, a lightweight content layer, an on-site AI chat assistant, and support.
8. **Human** reviews agent outputs during early validation, handles escalations, and makes strategic decisions.

This is not a custom-agency model. It is an AI-agent-operated productized service with selective customization. The money is in recurring retainers, not one-time builds. The leverage is in code — the agent scales without proportional human time.

## Value proposition

The offer solves five problems for small businesses at once:

- Their current site looks old, slow, or untrustworthy.
- They do not want to manage developers.
- They do not want to learn complicated tools.
- They want ongoing help rather than a one-off build.
- They want a modern feature set without hiring an internal team.

## Offer thesis

A large share of small local businesses either have no website, a broken website, or an outdated one that quietly destroys conversions. They often know the site is bad but delay action because typical web projects feel expensive, slow, and uncertain. The business removes that friction by showing a better version first and selling a low-friction monthly plan instead of a large upfront build.

## Revenue model

Primary revenue is recurring monthly subscription revenue. Secondary revenue comes from add-ons such as extra pages, extra chatbot training, seasonal landing pages, content entry, review widgets, or local SEO support.

## Why the model can work

- Prospecting is targeted rather than brand-led.
- The pitch is visual and specific rather than generic.
- The offer is easier to buy because the customer sees the improved site before committing.
- Delivery can be heavily templatized.
- Monthly retainers create compounding revenue.
- **An AI agent operates the business 24/7 at near-zero marginal cost per client.**
- **The 2-step sales motion (interest first, demo second) filters out tire-kickers before investing compute.**
- **Effective hourly rate for the human operator is $140–500+ instead of $20–40.**

## What is being sold

The client is not only buying a website. The client is buying a managed digital presence stack:

- Website design and front-end performance
- Hosting and uptime responsibility
- Basic content management access
- Visitor analytics visibility
- On-site AI chat assistant
- Ongoing edits and support
- A single accountable operator

## Who operates the business

| Layer | Operator | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Prospecting, auditing, outreach, demos | AI Agent | Google Maps scraping, site scoring, email sends, demo assembly |
| Onboarding, delivery, support, reporting | AI Agent | Template assembly, chatbot config, monthly reports, Tier 1 support |
| Payment setup, legal, compliance | Human | Dodo/Polar setup, LLC formation, tax filing |
| Quality checks, escalations, strategy | Human | Final pre-launch review, angry customers, niche decisions |
| Agent guardrails and prompt management | Human | What the agent can/cannot do, approval gates |

## What makes this different from a normal web agency

| Normal agency | This model |
|---|---|
| Starts with calls and discovery | Starts with one visible issue and a question |
| Charges big upfront project fees | Charges manageable recurring monthly fees |
| Custom builds every time | Uses niche templates and repeatable systems |
| Hands over site after launch | Stays in the loop as managed operator |
| Sells development | Sells business outcome and convenience |
| Human-dependent delivery | AI agent operates 24/7 at near-zero marginal cost |
| Shows demo to everyone | Demos only for interested prospects (saves compute, higher close rate) |

## Success conditions

For the model to work, the business must achieve all of the following:

- High-quality niche selection
- Consistent lead generation
- Strong demo or mockup quality
- Fast outreach cycles
- Clear pricing
- Tight fulfillment process
- Reliable recurring billing
- Low churn after month one

## Failure modes

The model breaks when:

- The AI agent produces low-quality customer-facing output (emails, demos, reports)
- Agent errors go undetected and compound (wrong content on live sites, spam complaints)
- Niches are too poor or too price-sensitive
- Outreach is too generic
- Fulfillment takes too much custom work
- Retainer promises exceed delivery capacity
- Agent makes promises outside scope (discounts, free months, custom work)
- Records and billing are not maintained properly
- The human operator does not maintain agent guardrails and quality oversight

## Business rules

- Do not sell everything to everyone.
- Do not start with many niches.
- Do not customize heavily before payment.
- Do not promise custom software in the base plan.
- Do not allow unclear deliverables.
- Do not scale outreach before fulfillment quality is stable.

## Recommended initial positioning

Initial positioning should emphasize a simple promise:

> A better website, managed for you every month, with hosting, analytics, chat assistant, and support included.

## Strategic objective

The short-term objective is to validate that the AI agent can produce customer-facing output that converts — close the first 5 clients, prove the agent works, then scale to 10. Formal US entity setup follows once retention and unit economics are proven.

The key gate is agent quality, not human capacity.
