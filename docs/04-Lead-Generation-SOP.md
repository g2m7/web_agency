# Lead Generation SOP

## Objective

Generate a steady stream of qualified businesses with bad or missing websites inside a narrow niche and geography.

## Core principle

Quality beats volume. A smaller list of highly obvious, highly relevant bad-site businesses will outperform a large generic list.

## Lead sources

Primary sources:

- Google Maps / Google Business listings
- Local directories
- Business websites found from maps searches
- Review platforms when relevant
- Business social profiles if website is missing

### Source compliance rule

Use scraping only for controlled validation batches. Before scaling a source, confirm that the acquisition method is permitted by the source's terms or replace it with an approved API, licensed provider, manual research, or first-party/partner data. Every lead must keep its `source` label so risky sources can be isolated or purged later.

## Lead data fields

Every lead record should capture:

- Business name
- Niche
- City / state
- Website URL
- Google Maps URL
- Email
- Phone
- Decision-maker name if available
- Audit score
- Key problems found
- Demo status
- Outreach status
- Last contact date
- Reply status
- Payment status
- Notes

## Prospecting process

1. Pick one approved city+niche pair from the scorecard.
2. Agent searches listings via Google Maps scraping or approved automation.
3. Agent opens each listing and site.
4. Agent applies the audit rubric and scores automatically.
5. Agent records only leads with clear visible problems.
6. Agent removes duplicates and obvious non-fits.
7. Agent prioritizes leads with reachable email and visible commercial intent.
8. Human reviews scored list periodically for quality assurance.

### Contact fallback rule (when email is missing)

- If no email is found but a clear phone number exists, keep the lead as **phone fallback** instead of excluding it.
- Default outreach still starts with email when available (2-step motion remains unchanged).
- Phone fallback leads can be routed to a short call/SMS opener only when email is not available.
- Track these separately from email-based outreach so channel performance can be measured.

## Exclusion rules

Exclude leads if:

- Website is already strong
- Business appears closed
- No useful contact path exists
- Business is a franchise with centralized control
- Business is likely managed by a larger corporate marketing team
- Business reputation is so poor that churn or disputes are likely

## Research depth rule

Do not over-research cold leads. The goal is enough information to create a relevant audit and pitch, not a full account dossier.

## Daily lead target

Recommended early target (agent-operated):

- 50–100 raw prospects reviewed per day by agent
- 20–40 qualified leads entered
- 10–20 high-priority leads advanced to outreach
- Human reviews agent's hot lead selection daily during early validation

## Data hygiene rules

- Standardize city/state names
- Normalize URLs
- Record the exact reason a lead was chosen
- Track outreach state meticulously
- Never email the same lead twice from separate rows

## Lead quality checklist

A lead is good when all or most are true:

- The site is visibly weak
- The business seems active
- The business has enough reviews or signs of demand
- There is a reachable contact method
- The value of one extra customer is meaningful
- The niche can use a reusable template

## Output standards

At the end of each prospecting cycle there should be:

- A cleaned lead list
- Audit scores attached
- A ranked shortlist of hot leads
- Notes explaining why each hot lead was selected
