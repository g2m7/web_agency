# Offer, Pricing, and Packages

## Pricing philosophy

The pricing model should optimize for fast buying decisions, predictable monthly cash flow, and manageable delivery. The subscription should feel cheaper than hiring staff, simpler than managing freelancers, and safer than paying a large upfront web project fee.

## Core package logic

Three packages are enough at the start:

| Package | Purpose | Good fit |
|---|---|---|
| Starter | Entry plan to reduce friction | Small local businesses with minimal needs |
| Growth | Main plan and expected best seller | Businesses that want a stronger presence and ongoing edits |
| Pro | Higher-touch managed plan | Businesses that need more speed, pages, and monthly support |

## Note on pricing viability

Because an AI agent handles delivery, support, and operations at near-zero marginal cost, these price points are economically viable even at the lower end. The main reason to price higher is client quality, not cost recovery — higher prices attract better clients who churn less.

## Draft package structure

### Starter
- 5-page website
- Mobile-optimized design
- Hosting included
- SSL and basic security setup
- On-site AI chat assistant with basic FAQ training
- Basic analytics dashboard
- Minor text/image edits each month
- Email support

### Growth
- Everything in Starter
- More page capacity
- Better chatbot setup and controls
- Basic CMS access for approved content fields
- Monthly content edits
- One landing page refresh or campaign update per month
- Priority support window

### Pro
- Everything in Growth
- Faster turnaround for edit requests
- Higher monthly change allowance
- Conversion-focused landing pages
- Advanced analytics summary
- Quarterly site improvement review
- Priority issue handling

## Suggested price anchors

The following range is intentionally conservative for early testing:

| Package | Indicative monthly price |
|---|---|
| Starter | $59–$79 |
| Growth | $99–$149 |
| Pro | $199–$299 |

Price should be tested against niche quality, close rate, and churn. If a niche is highly commercial and lead value is high, price can rise materially.

## Setup fee logic

Default recommendation:

- No setup fee for the earliest clients if the goal is proof of model.
- Introduce a setup fee only after the process is tight and case studies exist.
- Use setup fees primarily to protect time when customization rises.

## Scope boundaries

The base subscription should not include:

- Full custom software development
- Complex CRM integrations
- Unlimited design revisions
- SEO guarantees
- Ad management
- Logo/brand creation from scratch
- Copywriting from scratch unless explicitly sold

## Add-ons

Add-ons should be standardized and priced separately:

- Extra page creation
- Seasonal landing page
- Review or testimonial section refresh
- Additional chatbot knowledge upload
- Bulk content upload
- Emergency same-day changes
- Extra support hours
- Extra location pages

## Guarantee policy

A soft guarantee can reduce friction:

- Cancel anytime after the first billing cycle.
- If delivery cannot begin due to missing client materials, the subscription remains active unless a written exception is granted.
- Do not offer open-ended money-back guarantees unless margins and workflow can absorb abuse.

## Billing position

Billing should be simple:

- Monthly auto-renewing subscription
- One primary payment method on file
- Renewal attempts for failed payments
- Suspension after defined grace window
- Reactivation only after successful payment

## Offer framing for sales

Use language like:

- Better website, managed every month
- No big upfront project fee
- Hosting, analytics, chat assistant, and support included
- Fast launch with your existing business content
- Cancel anytime if it is not useful

## What must be consistent

The following must be identical across all sales material:

- Package names
- Monthly price points
- What is included
- What is excluded
- Edit limits
- Support window
- Cancellation terms
