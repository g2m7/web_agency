# Compliance and Records SOP

## Objective

Maintain enough documentation from day one so the business can prove what was sold, what was collected, what was delivered, and what records support those events.

## Guiding principle

Even before formal company formation, recordkeeping should operate as if the business will later be reviewed by an accountant, payment provider, or regulator.

## Minimum records to retain

For every client, keep:

- Sales messages and acceptance
- Package chosen
- Invoice or payment record
- Subscription ID or checkout proof
- Onboarding data
- Launch date
- Support history
- Cancellation or churn reason if applicable

## Folder structure

Recommended structure:

- `/records/clients/`
- `/records/contracts-or-acceptance/`
- `/records/invoices/`
- `/records/payouts/`
- `/records/remittance-proof/`
- `/records/support-logs/`
- `/records/reports/`

## Indian compliance hygiene

This section applies because the operator is based in India receiving inward remittances for services exported globally. Records relating to inward remittances and service export income should be kept in an organized way from the start because RBI/FEMA compliance depends heavily on documentation quality and the ability to show purpose, source, and receipt trail.[cite:76][cite:78]

Key points:
- All revenue is service export income (INR-denominated accounting may be needed for Indian tax purposes)
- Maintain purpose-of-remittance documentation for each payout
- Track FEMA-compliant documentation trail from contract/acceptance → invoice → payment → bank credit
- Consult a CA familiar with export services and inward remittances before first payout

## CAN-SPAM compliance

All outbound cold emails are B2B commercial communications and must comply with CAN-SPAM requirements:

### Requirements
1. **Physical address** — Include a physical mailing address in every email footer. A virtual office address is acceptable.
2. **Unsubscribe mechanism** — Every email must include a working opt-out method. A "reply STOP to unsubscribe" line or a link is sufficient. Opt-out requests must be honored within 10 business days.
3. **No deceptive subject lines** — Subject lines must accurately reflect the email content. Do not imply an existing relationship, urgency that doesn't exist, or mislead about the sender's identity.
4. **Clear sender identity** — The from name and address must identify the sender truthfully.

### B2B context
B2B emails have more flexibility than B2C marketing emails. The hook emails as written are one-to-one in tone and reference the prospect's actual business, which positions them closer to relationship-based outreach than bulk marketing. However, at scale (20+ emails/day), the safest posture is full compliance.

### Enforcement
- The agent must include the CAN-SPAM footer in every outbound email
- The agent must immediately suppress any lead who opts out
- The agent must log opt-outs in the lead record
- Never send to a lead who has previously opted out

## What to keep for each payout

- Payout amount
- Payout date
- Payment provider statement
- Bank credit proof
- Remittance / inward advice if available
- Mapping to one or more customer subscriptions

## Tax hygiene

Keep a running ledger with:

- Gross collections
- Fees deducted
- Net credited amount
- Refunds
- Month-wise revenue totals
- Business expenses

## Retention period

Maintain all operating and payment records for at least 6 years unless professional tax advice later requires longer retention.

## Audit readiness rule

If a transaction cannot be easily explained from lead to payment to delivery, the records are not good enough.
