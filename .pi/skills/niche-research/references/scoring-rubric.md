# Niche Scoring Rubric

## Dimension Definitions

### Website Pain Visibility (1–5)
How bad are the actual websites in this niche?

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 5 | 80%+ of sites are clearly bad (broken mobile, outdated, no CTA, placeholder) |
| 4 | 60–80% have obvious issues |
| 3 | 40–60% have issues, rest are adequate |
| 2 | 20–40% have issues, most are adequate |
| 1 | Most sites are decent to professional |

**How to measure:** Open 10 websites from Google Maps results in a test city. Count how many are clearly below average.

### Lead Value Per Customer (1–5)
How much is a single new customer worth to a business in this niche?

| Score | Lifetime customer value |
|---|---|
| 5 | $2,000+ |
| 4 | $1,000–2,000 |
| 3 | $500–1,000 |
| 2 | $200–500 |
| 1 | Under $200 |

**Why it matters:** Higher lead value = the website pays for itself faster = easier sell.

### Affordability / Retainer Fit (1–5)
Can a typical business in this niche comfortably pay $100–250/month?

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 5 | Annual revenue $500K+, owner easily affords $250/mo |
| 4 | Annual revenue $250–500K, can afford $150–250/mo |
| 3 | Annual revenue $100–250K, can afford $100–150/mo |
| 2 | Annual revenue under $100K, would feel the spend |
| 1 | Margins too thin for any recurring digital spend |

### Async Close Feasibility (1–5)
Can the owner buy from a cold email without meetings or committee approval?

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 5 | Solo owner decides immediately via email/text |
| 4 | Owner + 1 partner, fast decision |
| 3 | Small team, owner needs a day to think |
| 2 | Committee or board approval process |
| 1 | Enterprise procurement, RFP, legal review |

### Template Reusability (1–5)
How structurally similar are businesses in this niche?

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 5 | 95%+ structural overlap (same pages, sections, CTAs) |
| 4 | 80–95% overlap, minor variations |
| 3 | 60–80% overlap, some niche-specific pages |
| 2 | 40–60% overlap, significant customization needed |
| 1 | Every business is unique, templates don't work |

**Pages to check for overlap:** Homepage, About, Services, Contact, Gallery, FAQ, Service Areas

### Market Size (1–5)
How many potential businesses exist in the US?

| Score | Count |
|---|---|
| 5 | 50,000+ businesses |
| 4 | 20,000–50,000 |
| 3 | 10,000–20,000 |
| 2 | 5,000–10,000 |
| 1 | Under 5,000 |

### Growth Trajectory (1–5)
Is the industry growing, stable, or declining?

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 5 | 10%+ annual growth, new businesses opening constantly |
| 4 | 5–10% growth |
| 3 | Stable, 0–5% growth |
| 2 | Slow decline |
| 1 | Rapid decline / dying industry |

### Agency Competition (1–5)
How many agencies specifically target this niche?

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 5 | Zero specialized agencies found in search |
| 4 | 1–2 agencies, not aggressive |
| 3 | A few agencies, moderate competition |
| 2 | Multiple dedicated agencies, crowded |
| 1 | Oversaturated (like roofing/plumbing — dozens of agencies) |

### Support Complexity (1–5)
How much ongoing maintenance do these sites need?

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 5 | Very low — services rarely change, seasonal adjustments only |
| 4 | Low — occasional text/photo updates |
| 3 | Moderate — monthly changes expected |
| 2 | High — weekly updates, active promotions |
| 1 | Very high — daily changes, complex integrations |

**Note:** Higher support complexity isn't necessarily bad (means they need you), but it affects delivery cost and template design.

## Scoring Weight

Total score = sum of all 9 dimensions (max 45).

| Range | Classification |
|---|---|
| 36–45 | Strong pick — proceed with confidence |
| 28–35 | Viable — worth testing, has some risk |
| 20–27 | Marginal — significant concerns, needs strong justification |
| Under 20 | Skip — doesn't fit the model |

## Minimum Thresholds

Regardless of total score, a niche must meet ALL of these to be recommended:

- Website pain visibility ≥ 3 (sites must actually be bad)
- Lead value ≥ 3 (customers must be worth enough)
- Agency competition ≤ 3 (can't be oversaturated)
- Template reusability ≥ 4 (must be template-friendly)

If a niche fails any minimum threshold, flag it as a risk even if total score is high.
