01 — Overview

A complete, AI-agent-operated web agency. One human operator sets strategy. The AI agent handles prospecting, outreach, demo building, delivery, billing, and support 24/7.

The Core Loop

Every client follows the same path through this system:

02 — Strategy

Business Model

The agency sells a managed website retainer to local businesses with weak websites. Revenue is recurring monthly subscriptions, not one-time builds.

What the Client Buys

Not just a website. The client buys a complete managed digital presence:

Website Design & Performance

Mobile-first, fast-loading, conversion-focused site built from proven niche templates. Not a cookie-cutter — adapted to each business with their real content, services, and branding.

Hosting & Uptime

Deployed on Cloudflare Pages with global CDN, automatic SSL, and 99.9%+ uptime. The client never thinks about servers.

AI Chat Assistant

On-site chatbot trained on the client's FAQ data. Answers common customer questions 24/7. Falls back to human escalation when needed.

Analytics & Monthly Reports

Google Analytics 4 integrated. The agent generates a monthly performance summary showing traffic, key actions, and improvement suggestions.

Ongoing Edits & Support

Content updates, text changes, image swaps handled by the agent within defined monthly limits. Higher tiers get more edits and faster turnaround.

Single Accountable Operator

One point of contact. No handoffs between account managers. The agent handles routine; the human handles escalations and quality.

Revenue Model

Primary: Monthly recurring subscription revenue ($79–$249/mo per client).
Secondary: Standardized add-ons (extra pages, seasonal landing pages, emergency changes, extra chatbot training).

ⓘ
Key economic insight: Because the AI agent handles delivery, support, and operations at near-zero marginal cost, every additional client after the first is almost pure margin. The main reason to price higher is client quality and lower churn, not cost recovery.

Success Conditions

For this model to work, all of the following must be true:

  • High-quality niche selection (businesses that feel website pain)
  • Consistent lead generation (50–100 prospects/day reviewed)
  • Agent-written emails that get real replies (target: >5% reply rate)
  • Demos that convert (target: >30% demo-to-close)
  • Fast, predictable delivery (3–5 business days to launch)
  • Low month-1 churn (target: >80% retention)
  • Agent error rate below 5% on customer-facing interactions

04 — Strategy

Niche Discovery Engine

The system doesn't guess at niches. It probes real cities with real Google Maps queries and discovers what businesses actually thrive there. This is Step 0 — everything else depends on it.

How It Works

City Selection

The system maintains a database of 200+ US cities across three tiers: mid-size metros, smaller metros, and growing suburbs. Cities are probed in priority order.

Seed Data200+ Cities

Broad Probing

For each city, the agent sends broad Google Maps queries like "local services", "home services", "professional services". It doesn't use a fixed niche list — it discovers what categories Google Maps returns organically.

Organic DiscoveryGoogle Maps API

Category Harvesting

From the Maps results, the agent extracts real business categories. This reveals what actually thrives in each city — not what we assume should thrive.

Real Data

Auto-Scoring (100-Point Model)

Each city+niche pair is scored across five dimensions: demand signal, competition density, website weakness rate, contactability, and revenue potential. Pairs scoring 60+ are candidates.

Scored 0-10060+ = Candidate

Mini-Validation Scrape

Top-scoring pairs get a 30-lead test scrape. This confirms the niche actually has weak websites and reachable contacts before committing to a full outreach sprint.

30 LeadsValidation

Go/No-Go Decision

First 3 pairs require human approval via PATCH /api/niches/:id/approve. After 3 human reviews, auto-approve can be enabled. Approved pairs feed directly into the lead generation pipeline.

Human Gate (First 3)Auto-Approve Option

Scoring Dimensions

DimensionWhat It MeasuresWeight
Demand SignalHow many businesses exist in this niche in this city20 pts
Competition DensityHow many competitors are already serving this niche well15 pts
Website Weakness Rate% of sampled businesses with clearly bad websites25 pts

Email Enrichment

After scraping, each lead without an email goes through an enrichment phase. The agent visits the business's website and checks up to 5 pages (contact, about, footer, schema.org markup, mailto links) to find owner/decision-maker email addresses. Each found email gets a confidence score (high/medium/low) based on where it was found.

Exclusion Rules

Leads are automatically excluded if:

  • Their website is already strong (audit score < 4 out of 10)
  • The business appears permanently closed
  • It's a franchise with centralized marketing control
  • The business is clearly managed by a large corporate marketing team
  • The business reputation is so poor that churn/disputes are likely
  • No useful contact path exists (no email AND no phone)
⚠
Anti-detection: The scraper uses browser-mimicking headers, randomized delays between requests, and rate limiting (8 req/min for production scraping). It parses Google Maps hex place IDs directly. No API keys required.

07 — Pipeline

The 2-Step Sales Motion

The core innovation. The first email NEVER includes a demo. It asks one question. Only interested prospects get a demo + pricing. This saves compute, filters tire-kickers, and produces a higher close rate.

Why 2 Steps

Demo for EveryoneDemo on Request (Our Model)
Emails sent per batch100100
Demos built1003–5
Agent compute per lead~500 tokens~50 tokens (hook only)
Demo qualityGeneric (mass-produced)High (focused on interested prospect)
Close rate on demosLow (cold attachment)High (warm, engaged lead)
Tire-kicker filteringNoneBuilt-in to the process

Step 1: The Hook Email

Rules enforced by policy engine:

  • Must be under 80 words
  • Must reference one specific, visible issue from the audit
  • Must use the prospect's real business name and city
  • Must NOT include a demo link, preview URL, or pricing
  • Must end with one simple question
  • Must NOT insult the current site or use agency buzzwords

Follow-Up Sequence

Step 2: Demo + Pricing (Only on Positive Reply)

When a prospect replies "yes" or "interested", the agent:

  1. Classifies the reply with confidence score (>0.9 required)
  2. Triggers demo_build job automatically
  3. Builds a tailored demo using the niche template + prospect's real content
  4. Sends demo email with: demo link, what's included in the plan, price, checkout link

Billing States (Client Lifecycle)

StateMeaningNext Transitions

Failed Payment Workflow

  1. Renewal charge fails → agent sends brief notice to client
  2. Agent retries billing according to provider workflow (exponential backoff: 1h, 6h, 24h)
  3. If unresolved after retries → grace period (7 days)
  4. Agent suspends non-essential work after grace period
  5. Agent alerts human operator if client doesn't respond
  6. Agent cancels or reactivates based on final payment outcome

10 — Operations

Delivery & Launch

Every client site is built from a niche template + content swap. One JSON file per client. No WordPress, no CMS runtime, no server-side rendering. Static files deployed to Cloudflare Pages.

Template Architecture

niche-template/ ├── index.html # Homepage ├── about.html # About page ├── services.html # Services page ├── contact.html # Contact page ├── gallery.html # Gallery (optional per niche) ├── faq.html # FAQ page (chatbot training source) ├── assets/ │ ├── css/custom.css # Niche-specific overrides + client brand colors │ ├── js/chatbot.js # AI chat assistant widget │ └── img/ # Placeholder images replaced per client ├── data/ │ ├── content.json # ALL client-specific content (name, services, areas, testimonials, CTA config, contact info) │ └── faq.json # FAQ entries for chatbot training └── config.json # CTA type, analytics ID, chatbot ID, domain
ⓘ
Content swap mechanism: All client-specific text lives in data/content.json — not hardcoded in HTML. HTML templates use placeholders populated from the JSON at build time. Adding a new client = editing one JSON file + adding images. The agent never touches HTML.

Hosting & Domains

Cloudflare Pages

Zero-config SSL, global CDN, free tier covers early clients. Agent deploys via Git push or CLI. Custom domains via DNS CNAME. Target: <3s load on 4G mobile, Lighthouse >85.

Domain Ownership

Default: client owns their domain. Agent configures DNS CNAME. Exception: if client can't manage registration, agency registers on client's behalf with documented beneficial ownership.

Cancellation & Domain Transition

  1. Site remains live for 7 calendar days after cancellation (grace period for client to export content)
  2. Agent offers static content export upon request
  3. If client-owned domain: agent removes DNS records; client points domain wherever they choose
  4. If agency-registered domain: agent initiates transfer to client within 14 days
  5. After 7-day grace period: agent removes hosted site and staging preview
  6. All client assets marked as archived

17 — Controls

Risk Register

With an AI-agent model, risks shift from human capacity to agent quality, reliability, and guardrails. Every risk has a detection mechanism and a mitigation plan.

Priority Matrix

PriorityRiskDetectionMitigation
P0Agent produces low-quality customer-facing outputHuman review gates + spam complaint monitoringGradual gate removal only after KPIs proven; never batch-send without review in Phase 3
P0Agent data isolation errors (Client A content on Client B site)Automated data isolation check before every launchPolicy engine blocks launch; strict content separation in template architecture
P0Agent makes unauthorized promises (discounts, features, guarantees)Policy engine scans every outbound messageHard-coded guardrails; no override without human approval
P12-step sales motion fails (hook emails don't convert)Reply rate tracking per batchA/B test hooks; if reply rate <3%, revert to demo-in-first-email as backup
P1Wrong niche selectionZero or low reply rates from a niche sprint100-point scoring model + mini-validation before committing
P1Low hook email reply rate7-day rolling reply rate drops below 2%Sharpen issue selection, test subject lines, try SMS fallback channel
P2AI API cost scalingDaily cost tracking; alert if >2x rolling averageCheaper models for routine tasks; budget at <10% of revenue
P2Seasonal churn clusteringCohort analysis after 5+ clientsDiversify niches; offer pause option for seasonal businesses
P2Platform dependency (Google Maps, email provider, AI provider)Service degradation or API changesKeep processes modular; identify backup providers
